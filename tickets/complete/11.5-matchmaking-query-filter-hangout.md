### Found / done

**MAJOR — cross-ticket contamination in the implement commit (documented; not reverted, by
necessity).** Commit `acf00eb` accidentally swept in five `packages/db-core/src/reactivity/` files
that belong to the *unstarted* `tickets/implement/11.5-reactivity-backfill-resume-checkpoints`
ticket, not to matchmaking: new `backfill.ts`, `checkpoint.ts`, `wire-validate.ts`; plus a refactor
of `reactivity/wire.ts` and additions to `reactivity/config.ts`.
- `wire-validate.ts` + the `wire.ts` refactor + the `config.ts` additions are **load-bearing
  already** — the shipped, exported, *tested* reactivity wire codecs now import them, so a clean
  revert is impossible without breaking the 695 db-core tests. Left in place.
- `backfill.ts` and `checkpoint.ts` are **orphan** (not exported from `reactivity/index.ts`, no
  tests, and their Phase-1/2 handler + seam wiring are absent). Harmless (compiles, breaks nothing)
  but premature.
- Disposition: no new ticket (the work belongs to an existing implement ticket). Added a
  reconciliation heads-up block to that ticket so its agent finishes/exports/tests the drafts rather
  than re-creating them — including a flagged divergence (`BackfillReplyV1.available` on disk is
  `{ fromRevision, toRevision }` vs the ticket sketch's `{ from, to }`).

**MINOR — seeker never verifies the `QueryReplyV1` primary signature (observation; tracked, no fix
here).** `queryReplySigningPayload` is produced by `handleMatchmakingQuery` but has no seeker-side
consumer: `SeekerWalkClient.collect` re-validates each entry's `registrationSig` (the
security-critical check) but trusts `reply.topicTraffic` and the entry list unverified. Acceptable
for this slice — the reply is advisory and there is **no live transport binding yet** (the transport
seam is injected). When the protocol binds in `matchmaking-sweep-adversarial-module`, the seeker MUST
verify the reply sig before trusting `topicTraffic` (which drives the hang-out decision). Flagged
here so the requirement isn't lost; the handoff already called the broader "no live binding" gap.

**MINOR — asymmetry at the root with absent traffic (matches doc; left as-is).** When `topicTraffic`
is absent at `d = 0`, `handleAccepted` returns `terminal` immediately without spending the remaining
patience hanging out, whereas a normal escalate-at-root *does* hang out the remainder. This matches
doc §Edge cases 1 ("walks one tier toward the root **without** hanging out" / no estimation against
absent inputs) and is covered by the `missing topicTraffic at the root` test. Noted as a judgment
call; no change.

**MINOR — `decide()` is not re-run mid-hang-out (acknowledged in handoff; no fix).** Once hanging
out, the client polls until met or patience drains and does not re-evaluate feasibility on a
mid-hang-out traffic collapse. The doc doesn't require it (patience bounds the cost). No change.

**NIT — `filterAcceptRatio` numerator counts filter-AND-sig-verify passes over total returned**, so
a forged entry slightly depresses the ratio. Conservative and negligible (forgeries are rare and
already dropped); no change.

**Coverage notes (tests are solid; nothing blocking).** The `minBudget` exact-boundary case
(`capacityBudget === minBudget` passes the `>=`) is not asserted explicitly, though the logic is
trivial and the strictly-greater/strictly-less cases are tested. A `done` outcome at a non-root tier
leaves the registration to age out by TTL without a polite withdraw (only `escalate` withdraws) —
intentional and consistent with substrate TTL cleanup.
