# Complete: cohort-topic failure-mode operating-envelope boundaries

Two operating-envelope boundaries on top of the `findBoundary` harness, each bounding one
`ChurnRecoveryScenario` point-claim along a monotone-in-harm stress axis, driving the real
`ParticipantRenewal`/`TopicCohort` failover loop on the virtual clock:

- **Boundary 1** — `no-lost-registrations` × `killRatePerWindow`: members crashed per renewal window
  (`ttl/3`), staggered just after the window tick, from a single seeded priority order. Edge
  `k* ≈ 0.249`.
- **Boundary 2** — `heal-convergence` × `partitionSeverity`: split / concurrent-churn / heal; joins
  re-slot harmlessly, leaves remove a serving member. Edge `σ* ≈ 0.3115`, with a structural-trap
  guard proving the claim is not held up by the trivial `merge(a,b).epoch === pre.epoch` path.

Both reuse `TopicCohort`, `ParticipantRenewal`, `CohortMembership`, and the partition model unchanged.
New surface exported from `src/index.ts`; folded into a `Metrics` sink keyed by `(claim, axis)`.

- **Monotonicity argument verified.** Boundary 1: kills are drawn cursor-in-lockstep from one seeded
  Fisher–Yates order with a per-index stagger draw that is independent of the rate, so the dead set at
  a higher rate is a time-wise *prefix superset* of the lower rate's — genuinely monotone-in-harm.
  Boundary 2: the per-side leave order is a pure `fork(label)` permutation (seeded from the
  constructor seed, not live state — confirmed in `rng.ts`), so `leaving(σ1) ⊆ leaving(σ2)` for
  `σ1 < σ2`. Both nesting arguments hold.
- **Model correctness traced by hand** for both axes: the renewal ping schedule (`ttl/3` cadence,
  three-strikes promotion), the lazy `rotate`/`primary_moved` repoint, and the partition
  pre→split→late-churn→heal timeline. Boundary 2's non-converging participants are *exactly* the
  leave-victims (cached primary removed in the final pre-heal window, unreachable through heal, only
  `failures = 2` after one post-heal window — short of the 3 strikes a backup promotion needs).
  Window arithmetic matches `registration.ts` with no off-by-one.
- **Test-claim spot checks** confirmed against live sweeps: `k = 0.2` → `reachAtH = 4`, `holds`;

- **Probe-just-past-edge duplication** between `diagnoseKillMechanism` and `diagnosePartitionEdge`

### Major — filed as new ticket (not blocking)

- **`backlog/simulator-envelope-churn-richer-failover-model`** — both located edges are governed by
  structural *arithmetic* (Boundary 1: serving-capacity exhaustion `floor(k·N)·killWindows ≥ N`,
  seed-invariant; Boundary 2: `joinBudget` worth of severity) rather than by the renewal-timing race
  the boundaries were meant to stress. The renewal-race is real but transient in the current model.
  Making it the *binding* constraint needs a richer failover model (re-lookup latency/failure or a
  bounded re-lookup target set; a served-vs-unserved-member distinction for the partition axis). This
  is a modeling-fidelity enhancement the implementer flagged, not a delivered defect — **confirmed the
  serving-capacity reading is the honest one for the current model.** Filed to backlog (future
  concern) rather than blocking acceptance.
