# Crash-failover backup promotion vs. deterministic slot assignment

## Problem

The TTL-failover path (participant side) and the cohort-side renewal handler (`onRenew`) were
built and unit-tested independently with mock transports; they are **incoherent when composed**.

`docs/cohort-topic.md` §TTL and renewal specifies that after three consecutive ping failures the
participant promotes `backups[0]` to primary via a *re-attach RPC* (carrying the existing record,
no full re-registration), and "the backup verifies it sees the record in its local replica and
confirms."

In the delivered implementation:

- `RenewalParticipant.reattach()` sends a plain `RenewV1` — **identical** to a normal ping. There
  is no field distinguishing a re-attach from a renewal.
- The only cohort-side handler for an inbound `RenewV1` is `RenewalCohortSide.onRenew()`. It
  computes the deterministic `assignSlots(participant, cohortEpoch, members)` and, if `self` is not
  the computed primary, replies `primary_moved` pointing at the computed primary.
- On a *crash* (as opposed to a membership rotation) the `cohortEpoch` has **not** changed, so
  `assignSlots` still includes the dead member and still names it as the primary. The promoted
  backup is therefore *not* the computed primary.

Consequences:

1. A re-attach to `backups[0]` is answered with `primary_moved { newPrimary: <dead primary> }`.
   The participant's `failover()` happens to treat `primary_moved` as success and promotes the
   backup locally — but it **discards** the `primary_moved` payload, so it never learns the cohort
   disagrees.
2. On the *next* `pingLoop()` to the promoted backup, `onRenew` again replies
   `primary_moved { newPrimary: <dead primary> }`, and `applyPrimaryMoved` dutifully points the
   participant's `record.primary` back at the dead node — an indefinite bounce between the backup
   and the corpse until FRET membership rotation eventually changes the epoch.

Cross-member replication (ticket `cohort-topic-gossip-membership-certs`) gives the backup a copy of
the record, so it would stop returning `unknown_registration` — but replication alone does **not**
fix the redirect, because the backup still computes itself as not-the-primary under the unchanged
epoch.

## Design questions to resolve

- **How does a backup recognize a legitimate crash-failover promotion?** Options:
  - Add a re-attach discriminator to `RenewV1` (or a distinct `ReattachV1` message), and have
    `onRenew`/a new handler accept when `self ∈ record.backups` and the participant attests the
    primary is unreachable.
  - Have `onRenew` accept a renew whenever `self` is a backup for the record *and* the record is
    held locally, treating the deterministic primary as unreachable.
  - Keep slot assignment authoritative and lean entirely on fast membership rotation (epoch change)
    to reassign — i.e. drop the warm-backup fast path. (Likely unacceptable: the warm backup exists
    precisely to cover the crash→rotation gap.)
- **What stops two members both believing they are primary** during the window (the dead one if it
  revives, plus the promoted backup)? Tie-break / dual-serve semantics, analogous to the rotation
  handoff's `isServing`.
- **Should `failover()` honor a `primary_moved` payload** instead of blindly promoting the target?
  Today it ignores `newPrimary`/`newBackups`/`cohortEpoch` on the re-attach reply.

## Expected behavior

After a primary crash and three failed pings, a participant that re-attaches to `backups[0]` lands
on a cohort member that **accepts** the registration and serves it (touching `lastPing`,
optionally gossiping the promotion) without redirecting the participant back to the dead primary,
and the participant's subsequent pings remain stable on the new primary until a real membership
rotation supersedes it.
