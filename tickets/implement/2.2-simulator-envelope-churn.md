# Cohort-topic *failure-mode* operating-envelope boundaries

Two boundaries for the churn/partition failure modes (parent: `simulator-validity-envelope`, candidate
table rows "churn `no-lost-registrations`" and "churn `heal-convergence`"), driven by the
`findBoundary` harness from `simulator-envelope-core`. Reuse `TopicCohort`/`ParticipantRenewal`,
`CohortMembership`, `checkConvergence`, `ChurnGenerator` — new measurement, not new modeling.

> **Teeth.** The `ChurnRecoveryScenario` point-claims are partly structurally guaranteed today
> (`simulator-strengthen-scenario-adversariality` documents that `checkConvergence` is a pure
> determinism re-test and that all kills land at `t=0`). For a *boundary* to be meaningful the claim
> must be able to fail emergently along the axis. So both axes below drive **timed** failure against
> the renewal window — kills/partitions staggered on the virtual clock so failover races real renewal
> traffic — not a settled post-crash steady state. This is the same hardening the adversariality
> ticket calls for, applied here as the boundary's stress driver. Cross-ref, not prereq; design as if
> it lands.

## Boundary 1 — `no-lost-registrations` vs member-kill rate × timing

A participant's registration must survive cohort turnover via backup promotion / re-lookup within the
renewal window (`ttl/3` renewal cadence, three-strikes backup promotion). Past some kill rate — or
some adversarial *timing* relative to the renewal tick — failover exhausts backups or races renewal
and loses a registration.

- **axis** `killRatePerWindow` — members killed per renewal window (`ttl/3`), as a fraction of the
  cohort, staggered across the window via the seeded rng (monotone-in-harm). Optionally fold timing
  in by phasing kills just before vs just after the renewal tick; keep the *axis* one-dimensional
  (rate) with timing fixed to the worst phase so the boundary stays a clean curve.
- **claim** `no-lost-registrations` — `holds(k)` runs the cohort + renewals under that staggered kill
  rate and returns `lostRegistrations === 0` (reuse the `ChurnRecoveryScenario` aggregation:
  `reachable(primary)` over every renewal).
- **designAssumption** `killRatePerWindow = 0` (the design assumes turnover slower than the renewal
  window can absorb). Output: `k*` at first lost registration, `margin = k* − 0`; document that the
  margin is what the three-backup + `ttl/3` cadence buys.

## Boundary 2 — `heal-convergence` vs partition duration × concurrent churn

After a partition heals, every participant must re-converge on one deterministic primary within a
renewal window. With membership changing *during* the partition (so heal does not trivially reproduce
the pre-split member set), past some partition-duration × concurrent-churn severity the two sides
heal into a membership whose deterministic primary differs, or convergence takes longer than one
renewal window.

- **axis** `partitionSeverity` — a monotone-in-harm combination of partition duration (in renewal
  windows) and the count of membership changes applied during the partition. Increase severity by
  applying more concurrent member kills/joins per side while partitioned and holding the partition
  longer. (Define the axis as a single scalar that scales both, so it remains 1-D.)
- **claim** `heal-convergence` — `holds(σ)` splits the live membership, mutates each side under churn
  for the partition duration, heals, and returns whether every participant re-converges on the same
  primary within one renewal window (or the move is cleanly detected as `primary_moved` within the
  window — choose one and assert it; the structurally-trivial `epoch === pre.epoch` path must NOT be
  the only thing keeping it true).
- **designAssumption** `partitionSeverity = 0` (no concurrent churn, instant heal). Output: `σ*` at
  first non-convergence (or first window-overrun), `margin = σ* − 0`.

## Edge cases & interactions

- **Structural-guarantee trap.** If `holds` reduces to `CohortMembership.merge(a,b).epoch ===
  pre.epoch` (always true) the boundary is vacuous — `findBoundary` would report
  `boundaryFound=false` forever. Drive concurrent membership change so heal does *not* reproduce the
  pre-split set, and add a **positive control** (a severity known to break convergence) so the test
  proves the claim can fail.
- **Timing phase.** Kills staggered on the clock must straddle the renewal tick; pin the worst phase
  (kill a backup just as it would be promoted) so the boundary reflects the adversarial timing, not a
  lucky settle. Determinism: the stagger comes from the seeded rng only.
- **Backup exhaustion vs renewal race.** Boundary 1 can fail two ways (backups exhausted, or failover
  races renewal and loses); record which mechanism flipped the claim so the readout is diagnosable.
- **Partition both-sides asymmetry.** When membership churns asymmetrically across the two sides, heal
  may converge for some participants and not others — `holds` must be all-participants-converge, and
  the readout should record the converged fraction at the edge, not just a boolean.
- **Renewal-window units.** `ttl`, `ttl/3` renewal cadence, and "one renewal window" must be
  consistent across both axes; off-by-one in window accounting will move the boundary. Pin the window
  arithmetic and assert it in a test.
- **Determinism / no real time.** All kills, joins, and partition timings come from the seeded rng on
  the virtual clock; two runs at the same `(seed, config, axisValue)` must yield identical boundaries.
- **Cross-ref.** Shares drivers and hardening intent with
  `simulator-strengthen-scenario-adversariality`; design as if that lands, do not block.
