# Matchmaking operating-envelope boundaries

Two boundaries for matchmaking (parent: `simulator-validity-envelope`, candidate table rows
"matchmaking bounded-harm" and "matchmaking hang-out fairness"), driven by the `findBoundary` harness
from `simulator-envelope-core`. Reuse `SeekerWalk`/`TierProviderModel`/`TrafficReporter`,
`decideHangOut`/`contentionFactor`, `seekerPoolContentionWouldFlip` — new measurement, not new
modeling.

## Boundary 1 — bounded-harm vs lying-reporter fraction

A lying cohort primary mis-reports `topicTraffic`; the design claims bounded harm (under-report costs
`≤ +1` escalation hop per under-reported tier; over-report wastes `≤ patienceMs` of drain). As the
*fraction* of cohort reporters that lie grows — including a per-query-flipping adversary the seeker
cannot settle against — harm may stop being bounded.

- **axis** `lyingFraction ∈ [0, 1]` — fraction of reporters along the seeker's path that mis-report
  (monotone-in-harm). Include the per-query-flip variant from
  `simulator-strengthen-scenario-adversariality` (`over-report-bounded-drain` is near-tautological
  against a static lie; a per-query flip is the adversary with teeth) as the lie model at the swept
  fraction.
- **claim** `bounded-harm` — `holds(f)` runs honest vs adversarial `SeekerWalk`s (mirror
  `AdversarialReportingScenario`) at that lying fraction and returns whether the extra escalation hops
  stay `≤ +1 per under-reported tier` **and** wasted hang-out stays `≤ patienceMs`.
- **designAssumption** `lyingFraction = 0` (all-honest) — or the doc's tolerated single-liar point if
  matchmaking.md names one. Output: `f*` at which harm first exceeds the bound, `margin = f* − 0`;
  document that `patienceMs` / the `+1 hop/tier` structure is what bounds the harm up to `f*`.

## Boundary 2 — hang-out fairness vs seeker-pool contention ratio

The decision rule approximates competition as `queriesPerMin × meanWantCount` and clamps the
contention factor at `contention_factor_cap = 4`. As real seeker:provider contention rises past what
the cap models, the approximation diverges from the exact `Σ wantCount` and the hang-out decision
misfires (a seeker hangs out when it should escalate, or vice-versa).

- **axis** `seekerProviderRatio` — registered-seeker demand per provider (monotone-in-harm: more
  contention ⇒ the capped approximation increasingly understates true demand). Drive it via the exact
  `Σ wantCount` over a modeled seeker pool against a provider pool.
- **claim** `hang-out-fairness` — `holds(ρ)` returns whether the capped-approximation decision matches
  the exact-contention decision (reuse `seekerPoolContentionWouldFlip`: `holds = !wouldFlip`). The
  boundary is the ratio at which real contention first exceeds `contention_factor_cap` *and* that flips
  the decision.
- **designAssumption** the ratio at which `contentionFactor` first hits the cap of 4 — i.e. the cap is
  the design's assumed worst-case contention. Output: `ρ*` at which the decision first misfires,
  `margin = ρ* − ρ_cap`; this is the principled answer to whether
  `matchmaking-contention-from-seeker-pool` (the deferred exact-sum refinement) is actually needed.

## Edge cases & interactions

- **De-tautologize bounded-harm.** Against a *static* lie the `≤ patienceMs` bound is near-tautological
  (bounded by the seeker's own deadline). Use the per-query-flip adversary at the swept fraction so the
  claim can genuinely exceed the bound; positive control: a fraction/lie known to break it flips
  `holds` to false.
- **Cap-clamp degeneracy.** `contentionFactor` clamps at `contention_factor_cap`; below the cap the
  approximation and exact sum can already agree or disagree. The boundary must isolate the *flip* (the
  decision actually changes), not merely "the raw factor exceeded 4" — reuse
  `seekerPoolContentionWouldFlip` so the predicate is the decision flip, and guard the
  `arrivalsPerMin = 0` divide path (the `max(arrivalsPerMin, 1)` guard already in `contentionFactor`).
- **Per-tier vs path harm.** Under-report harm is `+1 per under-reported tier`; the lying-fraction
  axis spreads liars across tiers, so the bound is per-tier-summed, not a flat constant — compute the
  expected bound from the number of lied-about tiers at each fraction, not a fixed number.
- **Seeker own-deadline coupling.** Wasted-drain is bounded by `patienceMs + setup`; ensure the
  boundary measures harm relative to the honest baseline (extra hops / extra drain), not absolute
  latency, so the bound isn't trivially satisfied by the deadline.
- **Determinism.** `SeekerWalk` and the seeker pool draw from seeded worlds/rng; two runs at the same
  `(seed, config, axisValue)` must give identical boundaries.
- **Cross-ref.** `simulator-strengthen-scenario-adversariality` (per-query-flip hardening),
  `matchmaking-contention-from-seeker-pool` + `matchmaking-per-tier-patience-splitting` (the deferred
  refinements these boundaries quantify the need for). Design as if they land.
