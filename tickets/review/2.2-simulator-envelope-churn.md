# Review: cohort-topic failure-mode operating-envelope boundaries

Two new operating-envelope boundaries were added on top of the committed `findBoundary` harness
(`simulator-envelope-core`), bounding the two `ChurnRecoveryScenario` point-claims along
monotone-in-harm stress axes. Both drive the **real** `ParticipantRenewal`/`TopicCohort` failover
loop on the virtual clock — kills/partitions staggered on the renewal cadence — so the edge reflects
timing, not a structural identity. Build is clean (`tsc` strict, src+test) and all **227** package
tests pass (11 new).

## What landed

`packages/substrate-simulator/src/boundary-churn.ts` — mirrors `boundary-tree.ts`: per-axis
`BoundaryAxisSpec` builders + `holds` evaluators, public `measure*` diagnostics, a `runChurnBoundaries`
driver that folds each `EnvelopeBoundary` into a `Metrics` sink via `recordBoundary`, plus edge
diagnostics. New surface exported from `src/index.ts`.

### Boundary 1 — `no-lost-registrations` × `killRatePerWindow`
- **axis**: members crashed per renewal window (`ttl/3`) as a fraction of the cohort, sustained over
  `killWindows`, drawn from a single seeded priority order (so a higher rate's dead set is a time-wise
  superset — monotone), each kill staggered *just after* the window-opening tick (worst phase: a crash
  is unseen until the next ping a full window later).
- **claim**: `lostRegistrations === 0` — the `ChurnRecoveryScenario` aggregation (`!reachable(primary)`
  over every participant), snapshotted `settleWindows` (= 4 > 3 strikes) after the last kill.
- **measured edge** (default seed 4242): `k* ≈ 0.249`, `margin ≈ 0.249`, `boundaryFound`, not
  monotone-violated.

### Boundary 2 — `heal-convergence` × `partitionSeverity`
- **axis**: a `[0,1]` severity scaling the per-side concurrent-churn count. The first `joinBudget` (2)
  changes are *joins* (re-slot the deterministic primary but stay reachable → lazy `primary_moved`),
  beyond which they are *leaves* (remove a serving member from the healed set). Churn lands in the
  **final** pre-heal window (recent, racing the one-window repoint).
- **claim**: every participant on both sides repoints to the healed deterministic primary
  (`renewal.primary === H.assign(pid).primary`) within **one** renewal window. A participant whose
  served primary was removed must instead run a multi-window backup promotion and misses the bound.
- **measured edge**: `σ* ≈ 0.312`, `margin ≈ 0.312`, `boundaryFound`, not monotone-violated; converged
  fraction degrades `1.0 → 0.16` across the axis.
- **structural-trap guard (important)**: below the edge the healed membership *already changed*
  (`healedEpochChanged === true`) yet every participant still converges — proving the claim is NOT held
  up by the trivial `merge(a,b).epoch === pre.epoch` path. It breaks only when a *leave* removes a
  served member. The spec asserts this directly.

## Honest gaps / where to push (treat this as a floor, not a finish line)

- **Boundary 1's edge is serving-capacity exhaustion, not the renewal-race.** This is a faithful
  consequence of the model: `ParticipantRenewal.reLookup` recovers from *any* still-reachable member
  within three strikes, so below full-cohort exhaustion every crash recovers and the snapshot
  `lostRegistrations` stays 0. The edge therefore sits where sustained kills drive the cohort to
  `reachableMembersAtHorizon === 0` (≈ the `floor(k·N)·killWindows ≥ N` window arithmetic, measured
  through the live loop). The renewal-race **is** real but transient — captured separately as
  `peakTransientLost` (≈ 46 of 80 participants momentarily lost at `k=0.2` while the snapshot still
  holds). Consequently `killMechanism` at the edge is always `backup-exhaustion`; the `renewal-race`
  branch of the *snapshot* mechanism is effectively unreachable in this model. A reviewer who wants the
  race to be the binding constraint would need a richer model (re-lookup latency/failure, or a bounded
  re-lookup target set) — candidate follow-up, not a defect here. Confirm you agree the
  serving-capacity reading is the honest one for the current model.
- **Boundary 2 folds partition *duration* into the change-count** rather than scaling it as a separate
  clock dimension: severity scales concurrent membership-changes-per-side (= duration × rate), and the
  churn is applied in the single final pre-heal window so it genuinely races the one-window repoint.
  The two sides are simulated as independent single-cohort `rotate` sequences (pre → side → churned →
  healed) sharing the merged `H`, exploiting the lazy-rotation model (`partition.spec.ts` pattern), not
  two concurrently-live network-partitioned cohorts. Crashes are modeled as membership *leaves*
  (stabilized out of `H`) to avoid `rotate`'s revive-on-readmit; a dead-but-still-listed member is not
  exercised. Check this is a fair stand-in for "the deterministic primary differs after heal."
- **`σ*` is largely deterministic given `joinBudget`** (the first leave past the join budget strands
  the ~4 participants that member served, since `participantCount > memberCount`). The margin is
  essentially `joinBudget` worth of severity — i.e., "how much re-slotting churn heal absorbs before a
  served-member removal bites." Reasonable, but less emergent than Boundary 1's; consider whether a
  served-vs-unserved-member distinction would make `σ*` more interesting.
- **Adversarial-timing knob is fixed, not swept.** Kills are staggered across the window via the seeded
  rng (worst phase = just after the tick); the boundary does not separately sweep the kill *phase* as a
  second dimension. The axis is kept 1-D by design (per the ticket), but a reviewer may want a
  spot-check that a different phase doesn't materially move `k*`.
- Shares hardening intent with `simulator-strengthen-scenario-adversariality` (cross-ref, not prereq).
