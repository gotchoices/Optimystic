description: Mechanism to reverse committed transactions that are proven invalid through dispute resolution
prereq: right-is-right-dispute-protocol
----

When the dispute protocol resolves in favor of the challenger (the majority was wrong), the committed transaction needs to be invalidated.  This is the most complex piece of the right-is-right architecture and warrants its own design round.

Key questions to resolve:

* **Cascade scope**: Subsequent transactions may have read data written by the invalidated transaction (read dependencies).  These must also be invalidated.  How deep can this cascade go?  Is there a practical bound?
* **Log representation**: The collection log records committed transactions.  Options include: a) append a compensating "invalidation" entry; b) mark the log entry as void; c) rewrite the log (dangerous).  Option (a) preserves the audit trail.
* **Concurrent access**: While invalidation is in progress, reads and writes to the affected collections must be handled.  Should the collection be briefly locked?  Or should invalidation be eventually-consistent?
* **Client notification**: Clients that received "committed" status need to learn about invalidation.  Push notification (if connected) vs. pull (next status query).
* **Partial invalidation**: If a multi-collection transaction is invalidated, all collections are affected.  But some collections may have had subsequent non-dependent transactions that are fine.
* **Reapplication**: After invalidation, can the cascade be partially reapplied?  (i.e., transactions that didn't actually depend on the invalidated data, despite appearing in the cascade chain.)
